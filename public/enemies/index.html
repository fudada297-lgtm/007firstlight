007 First Light — All Enemy Types & How to Counter Them

007 First Light features a variety of enemy types, each with distinct behaviors and weaknesses. Knowing what you're up against is half the battle. Here's the complete breakdown.

Standard Enemies

Thugs (Unarmed / Melee)

Untrained mercenaries who rely on numbers rather than skill. They're dangerous in groups because they can surround Bond quickly. Use attack-throw combinations to keep them at range and prevent being encircled. Perfect parry windows are generous against these enemies — practice your timing on them.

Strategy: Pick them off one by one. Stealth takedowns work perfectly. In open combat, use the environment — fire extinguishers, chandeliers, and ledges are everywhere.

Armed Guards (Pistol)

The most common enemy type. They carry standard pistols and use basic cover tactics. Individually they're not threatening, but they coordinate and call for backup. Their aim improves significantly at close range.

Strategy: Shoot weapons from their hands for a disarm (unlocks the Sleight of Hand trophy). Headshots are one-hit kills. Watch for the yellow antenna icon — they love calling reinforcements.

Security Personnel (SMG / Shotgun)

Better-trained than street thugs. Security guards carry SMGs or shotguns and use more advanced tactics — they flank, suppress, and use flashbangs. Shotgun variants are deadly at close range; SMG variants lay down sustained fire.

Strategy: Shotgun guards have a slow rate of fire — dodge between shots and close the distance. SMG guards are vulnerable to flanking. Hack their grenades with the Q-Watch.

Heavy Units

Armored Enforcers

Heavily armored soldiers who absorb significant damage. They carry assault rifles or shotguns and can't be taken down with standard melee attacks from the front. Headshots require multiple hits due to helmets.

Strategy: Attack from behind for stealth takedowns. In direct combat, use the Laser Strap to stun them, then follow up with a takedown. Missile Pen and Shockwave Camera are effective. Environmental kills (ledges, explosives) bypass armor entirely.

Riot Shield Guards

Soldiers carrying ballistic shields that block all frontal gunfire. They advance slowly and protect allies behind them. Shooting the shield does nothing — you need to get creative.

Strategy: Flank them for a clear shot. Use Smoke Pods to break line of sight and reposition. The Dart Phone can hit exposed limbs. A thrown object followed by a quick flank works well.

Special Units

Snipers

Positioned on elevated perches with long-range rifles. Their laser sights give away their position, but they can one-shot kill Bond on higher difficulties if you stand still too long.

Strategy: Stay in cover and move between blind spots. Use Q-Lens to mark their position through walls. Close the distance using indoor routes. Alternatively, counter-snipe them with a scoped weapon if you have one.

Technicians / Hackers

Non-combat specialists who deploy drones, turrets, and alarm systems. They're physically weak but can make a fight much harder if left unchecked. They typically stay in the back line during combat.

Strategy: Eliminate them first in any engagement. Stealth takedowns are ideal since they're usually isolated. If an alarm goes off, find and disable the technician who triggered it.

Attack Dogs

Guard dogs that can smell through disguises and track Bond's scent. They alert nearby guards and can pin Bond down, leaving him vulnerable to enemy fire.

Strategy: Dogs can't be bluffed. Avoid their patrol paths entirely, or use the Dart Phone's sleeping darts for a silent takedown. In combat, a single well-placed shot handles them, but it alerts everyone nearby.

Bosses & Elite Enemies

009 (Rogue Agent)

A rogue MI6 agent and one of the game's primary antagonists. Trained with the same skillset as Bond — expect counters, parries, and gadget usage. The fight emphasizes one-on-one combat mechanics.

Strategy: This is a test of your core combat skills. Time your parries carefully. Use Focus mode for precision shots during openings. Don't rely on gadgets — 009 can counter most of them.

Tero Murto

A close-quarters assassin encountered in the London museum basement (Chapter 11). Uses environmental traps and stealth attacks. The fight takes place in a multi-room basement with destructible elements.

Strategy: Use Q-Lens to track his movements through walls. Environmental hazards (chandeliers, electric floors, exhaust pipes) can be triggered to damage him. Don't stay in one room too long — keep moving.

Niko Murto

Tero's brother, encountered later in the story. Similar fighting style but with additional ranged attacks and more aggressive AI. The fight arena typically has more verticality.

Strategy: Similar to Tero, but Niko is more aggressive and harder to track. Use elevation changes to your advantage. The Laser Strap can interrupt his ranged combos.
General Combat Reminder: If you're overwhelmed, run. Breaking line of sight and repositioning is always better than fighting at a disadvantage. Health regen is slow — survival beats heroics in 007 First Light.
