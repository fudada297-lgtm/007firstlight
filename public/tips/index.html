007 First Light — 14 Essential Tips & Tricks for Beginners

These tips will help you master stealth, combat, and gadget usage in 007 First Light. The game shares DNA with IO Interactive's Hitman series — many tactics carry over.

1. Listen to Enemy Chatter

Enemies call out their tactics verbally — flanking maneuvers, grenade throws, calling for backup. Pay attention to their dialogue. If you hear "He's throwing a grenade!", you can interrupt the thrower or hack the live grenade with your Q-Watch to disable it.

2. Prioritize Enemies Calling for Backup

Watch for the yellow antenna icon above an enemy's head. This means they're radioing for reinforcements. Neutralize them before the bar fills, or you'll face a much tougher fight.

3. Use Q-Lens Constantly

Activate Q-Lens (the scanning vision mode) frequently. It highlights: hidden keys, collectibles, NPC outlines through walls, hackable devices, and gadget interaction points. It's the single most useful tool in the game. Get in the habit of toggling it every time you enter a new area.

4. Check Challenges at the Start of Every Chapter

Each mission has multiple challenge objectives (stealth, combat, exploration). Open the Challenges menu at the start of each chapter. Knowing what's required lets you plan your approach — some challenges require ghost runs, others need specific takedown methods.

5. Use the Checkpoint System for Cleanup

Chapter Select unlocks after you finish the game. You can replay from any checkpoint to clean up missed challenges, collectibles, and trophies. Nothing is permanently missable. Don't stress about getting everything on your first playthrough.

6. Button Mashing Will Get You Killed

Unlike many action games, enemies in 007 First Light attack simultaneously. Spamming attack buttons is dangerous. Use calculated strikes, parries, and sidesteps. Watch for the yellow glint — when it reaches full brightness, that's your window for a perfect parry.

7. Headshots and Leg Shots Matter

Headshots kill unarmored enemies in one hit. Leg shots incapacitate enemies without killing them — essential for non-lethal playthroughs and certain challenges. Aim deliberately rather than spraying.

8. Pick the Right Gadgets Before Each Mission

You can customize your gadget loadout at Q-Lab or at the start of each mission. General rule:

PlaystyleRecommended Gadgets
Stealth / GhostDart Phone, Smoke Pod, Laser Strap, Q-Watch
Loud / CombatMissile Pen, Shockwave Camera, Flash Mine Earphones

9. Cover Is Destructible

Wooden crates and sheet metal break quickly under sustained fire. Look for metal beams, concrete pillars, and solid walls when you need reliable cover. Don't get caught behind a wooden box in a prolonged firefight.

10. Low Health? Run and Create Distance

Health regeneration is slow and unforgiving in 007 First Light. When you're low, don't try to tank through it. Break line of sight, sprint around obstacles, and create distance. Use Smoke Pods to cover your retreat.

11. Master the "License to Kill" Mechanic

You can only use lethal force once enemies draw their weapons first. Before that, stick to non-lethal takedowns. This isn't just a story gimmick — it's a core gameplay constraint. Learning when lethal force unlocks is key to efficient combat.

12. The Q-Watch Has Two Resource Meters

The Q-Watch runs on two separate resources: Electronic (recharged from batteries, car cells, power outlets) and Chemical (refilled from cleaning supplies, hand sanitizer, lab equipment). If one gadget stops working, check which resource you need. Explore thoroughly to keep both meters topped up.

13. Bluffing Actually Works

When guards get suspicious, you can talk your way out using the Bluff system. But be warned: you must remember your cover story for follow-up questioning. If you said you're a journalist, a guard might ask which publication — and getting it wrong blows your cover.

14. Empty Guns Are Still Useful

An empty firearm can be thrown at an enemy to stagger them, creating an opening for a takedown or escape. This works with any gun, not just pistols. It's a great panic-button move when you're out of ammo.

Bonus Tip: Play through the main story first without worrying about collectibles or challenges. Enjoy the narrative, learn the systems, then use Chapter Select for cleanup. The game is designed for replayability — your second run will be much smoother.
